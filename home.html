Cars are everywhere in America. We build our cities and suburbs around the use of the car. As the most common way to get around, federal, state, and city governments spend millions of tax dollars in infrastructure for the car. However, this has led to detrimental effects on our towns and cities.

Our overreliance on the automobile is perpetually increasing money pit for tax dollars on continual repairs of roads and highways, spread out our cities into vast sprawls, limiting movement of the lower class who can not affored cars from important job opportunities, locking them in their communities. As well as diverting funds from alternative forms of transportation, creating massive space issues for cities with parking, producing an immense percentage of CO2 emissions, and are greatly unsafe for anyone else using the roads that belong to all of us.

EFFECTS ON OTHER MODES OF TRANSPORT

The overfunding on car infrastructure has diverted vital capital away from other forms of transportation in our cities. Many cities throughout America have inadequate bus services with few routes, and most cities do not have rapid train transit at all.
